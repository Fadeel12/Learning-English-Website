Learn Tenses

Tenses are a part of English grammar used to express time. We will discuss the following types of tenses:

    Present Tenses

    Present tenses are used to express something that is happening now. There are three types of present tenses, which are:

    • Present Simple: used to express something that happens regularly or is a habit. Example: I go to the gym every day.
    • Present Continuous: used to express something that is currently happening. Example: I am reading a book right now.
    • Present Perfect: used to express something that has happened in the past and is still relevant to the present. Example: I have visited Paris twice.

  • Past Tenses

    Past tenses are used to express something that has happened in the past. There are three types of past tenses, which are:

    • Past Simple: used to express something that happened once or several times in the past. Example: I went to the gym yesterday.
    • Past Continuous: used to express something that was happening in the past. Example: I was reading a book when you called.
    • Past Perfect: used to express something that had happened before another past action. Example: I had finished my homework before my friend came over.

  • Future Tenses

    Future tenses are used to express something that will happen in the future. There are several ways to express future tenses, including:

    • Will: used to express a decision made at the time of speaking. Example: I will go to the store later.
    • Going to: used to express a plan or intention. Example: I am going to visit my family next week.
    • Present Continuous: used to express a future arrangement or timetable. Example: I am leaving for the airport at 5 pm.

  • Present Perfect Continuous

    Present perfect continuous is used to express an action that started in the past and continues up to the present. Example: I have been studying for hours.

  • Past Perfect Continuous

    Past perfect continuous is used to express an action that started in the past and continued up to another past action. Example: She had been living in New York for five years when she moved to Los Angeles.

  • Future Perfect Continuous

    Future perfect continuous is used to express an action that will start and continue up to a future time. Example: By this time next year, I will have been working here for two years.

  • Present Perfect

    Present perfect is used to express an action that started in the past and has been completed in the present. Example: I have finished my homework.

  • Past Perfect

    Past perfect is used to express an action that was completed before another past action. Example: She had already eaten when I arrived.

  • Future Perfect

    Future perfect is used to express an action that will be completed before a future time. Example: I will have finished my project by tomorrow.

  • Present Subjunctive

    Present subjunctive is used to express a hypothetical or unlikely situation in the present. Example: It's essential that he study harder.

  • Past Subjunctive

    Past subjunctive is used to express a hypothetical or unlikely situation in the past. Example: If I were taller, I could reach the top shelf.

  • Future Subjunctive

    Future subjunctive is used to express a hypothetical or unlikely situation in the future. Example: If she were to win the lottery, she would buy a new house.

  • Present Unreal

    Present unreal is used to express an unreal or impossible situation in the present. Example: If I had a million dollars, I would buy a new car.

  • Past Unreal

    Past unreal is used to express an unreal or impossible situation in the past. Example: If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

  • Future Unreal

    Future unreal is used to express an unreal or impossible situation in the future. Example: If I won the lottery, I would buy a new house.
